# Firewood Books

A simple, offline bookkeeping app for a small firewood business.

## How to use it

Double-click `index.html` to open it in your web browser. That's it — no installation, no internet connection, no account, no monthly fees.

All your data is saved in this browser, on this computer, using its local storage. It stays there between visits automatically — you don't need to save anything manually.

## Back up your data

Since everything lives only in this browser, **back up your data regularly**:

1. Go to **Backup & Settings**.
2. Click **Export Backup File** — this saves a single file with everything (sales, expenses, mileage, inventory).
3. Keep a copy somewhere safe (a USB drive, a cloud folder, emailed to yourself, etc.).

If you ever clear your browser's data, switch computers, or something goes wrong, use **Import Backup File** on that same page to restore everything from your last backup.

## Sample data

The app opens with a few sample sales, expenses, mileage trips, and wood batches already entered, so you can see how everything works before you use it for real. When you're ready, go to **Backup & Settings → Clear All Data & Start Fresh**.

## What this app does — and doesn't do

This app helps you keep organized, consistent records for a sole-proprietor firewood business (Schedule C). It does **not** calculate taxes owed, tell you what's deductible, or give tax advice — it just organizes your numbers so you or a tax preparer can use them. Please check with a tax professional or your state's Department of Revenue about your specific obligations, including whether firewood sales are taxable in your state.

## Files

- `index.html` — the app
- `style.css` — styling
- `app.js` — all the logic and data handling

No build step, no dependencies, no framework — just open the file. It should keep working for as long as web browsers exist.
